Awards and Recognition

Chemference 23

Jinious Sheth and the Sumacom Consultancy's team at IIT-Gandhinagar presented their research on environmental remediation at Chemference 23, a prestigious national chemical engineering conference. Their work focuses on repurposing waste EPS (Expanded Polystyrene) into a biodegradable adhesive, offering a sustainable alternative to conventional adhesives.

This innovation not only helps in reducing plastic waste but also promotes eco-friendly industrial applications in packaging, construction, and furniture. The recognition at Chemference 23 further validates the impact of this research in the field of sustainable materials and chemical engineering.

Undergraduate Research Showcase at IIT Gandhinagar

It also secured the 2nd Best Poster Prize at the Undergraduate Research Showcase held at IIT Gandhinagar, where 100 undergraduate students presented their research.

Boeing BUILD 3.0

Representing Sumacom Consultancy Pvt. Ltd, Jinious Sheth participated in Boeing BUILD 3.0, where 1200 startups showcased their ideas. He made it to the regional finals and emerged as one of the top 4 finalists, further securing a spot among the top 28 startups nationwide. This achievement highlights the significance and potential impact of the biodegradable adhesive in promoting sustainability.

Featured in Gujarati Newspaper

The innovative research on converting waste EPS into a biodegradable adhesive was recently featured in a leading Gujarati newspaper.

  • Researchers at Sumacom Consultancy have developed a method to convert waste thermocol into an eco-friendly adhesive that can be used as a sustainable alternative.
  • The biodegradable adhesive (eco-adhesive) dissolves in natural conditions instead of remaining as non-degradable waste.
  • It can be used in furniture, packaging, and construction industries, providing a sustainable solution.
  • The adhesive formula is based on chemical engineering principles and can reduce dependence on synthetic adhesives.
